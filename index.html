About Me

Hello! I am Purushothaman Manivannan, a PhD Researcher at the University of Antwerp in Belgium. I am a part of the Computational Materials Modeling (COMMIT) Lab led by Dr. Cem Sevik. My research focuses on excitonic calculations and the computational modeling of two-dimensional (2D) materials.

Previously, I worked as a Junior Research Fellow at the Materials Modeling and Simulation Laboratory at IIITDM Kancheepuram under the guidance of Dr. Debolina Misra. During this time, I explored the impact of defects, strain, and doping on the optoelectronic properties of strongly correlated oxide materials using Density Functional Theory (DFT).

Research

My expertise includes DFT calculations using GGA-PBE and DFT+U methods implemented in VASP. I focus on optimizing optical and transport properties in strongly correlated materials for next-generation transparent conducting applications.

Recently, I presented my previous work during the COMMIT Seminar through a talk titled "Empirical DFT Analysis of d-Orbital Band Engineering in Gapped Metallic Oxide SrVO₃". This study examined how biaxial strain and transition-metal doping (3d, 4d, 5d series) can engineer the optoelectronic properties of SrVO₃, a promising indium-free transparent conductor material.

In addition, I am proficient in using Python for post-processing VASP outputs, employing tools like VASPKIT and BoltzTraP to analyze electronic, optical, and transport properties.
