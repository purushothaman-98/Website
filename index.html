About Me

Hello! My name is Purushothaman Manivannan. I am currently a PhD Research Scholar at the University of Antwerp, working in the Computational Materials Modeling (COMMIT) Lab under the supervision of Dr. Cem Sevik. My research focuses on excitonic calculations and the modeling of 2D materials using advanced computational techniques.

Previously, I spent two wonderful years as a Junior Research Fellow at the Materials Modeling and Simulation Laboratory at IIITDM Kancheepuram, working under the guidance of Dr. Debolina Misra. During this time, I developed strong expertise in computational materials science, particularly Density Functional Theory (DFT) studies of correlated oxide materials.

Research

My experience includes working with DFT functionals (including GGA-PBE) and the DFT+U method within the Vienna Ab initio Simulation Package (VASP) to accurately compute electronic structures. I have optimized optical and transport properties in strongly correlated oxides to develop cost-effective transparent conducting materials.

One of my key projects involved investigating the effect of biaxial strain and transition-metal doping (3d, 4d, 5d series) on the optoelectronic properties of the gapped metallic oxide SrVO3. This work identified the potential of SrVO3 as a promising candidate for indium-free transparent conductors.

As part of my initial introduction at University of Antwerp, I delivered a seminar titled "Empirical DFT Analysis of d-Orbital Band Engineering in Gapped Metallic Oxide SrVO₃", where I presented my findings and received valuable feedback from the COMMIT team.

Beyond DFT calculations, I also extensively use Python for post-processing and visualizing VASP outputs, utilizing tools such as VASPKIT and BoltzTraP. I have also worked on d-band center analysis for CO2 reduction reactions, broadening my research exposure.
