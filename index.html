About Me

Hello! I am Purushothaman Manivannan, a dedicated PhD Researcher at the University of Antwerp in Belgium. I operate within the Computational Materials Modeling (COMMIT) Lab under the supervision of Dr. Cem Sevik. My primary research focus encompasses excitonic calculations and the computational modeling of two-dimensional (2D) materials.

Previously, I served as a Junior Research Fellow at the Materials Modeling and Simulation Laboratory at IIITDM Kancheepuram under the guidance of Dr. Debolina Misra. During this tenure, I explored the impact of defects, strain, and doping on the optoelectronic properties of strongly correlated oxide materials using Density Functional Theory (DFT).

Research Focus

My technical expertise lies in DFT calculations utilizing GGA-PBE and DFT+U methods implemented in VASP. I specialize in optimizing optical and transport properties in strongly correlated materials to advance next-generation transparent conducting applications.

Recently, I delivered a talk titled "Empirical DFT Analysis of d-Orbital Band Engineering in Gapped Metallic Oxide SrVO₃" during the COMMIT Seminar. This study examined how biaxial strain and transition-metal doping (3d, 4d, 5d series) can engineer the optoelectronic properties of SrVO₃, a promising indium-free transparent conductor material.
